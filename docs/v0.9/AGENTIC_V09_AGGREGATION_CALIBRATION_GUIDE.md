# Agentic v0.9 Labelwise Aggregation and Calibration Guide

This guide explains how v0.9 evaluates parent-level aggregation and threshold calibration for the human-talk multi-label audio task.

v0.9 uses the v0.8-HCB trained model and performs post-hoc analysis on the corrected holdout. It does not retrain the model.

---

## 1. Input and output

The evaluator starts from segment-level probabilities:

```text
parent_clip_id
<label_1>, <label_2>, ...
exit3_prob_<label_1>, exit3_prob_<label_2>, ...
```

The main input file is:

```text
human_talk_workspace\tata_v0.9_labelwise_calibration\verification\v08_parent_mean_fixed\parent_eval_segment_probs_fixed_0p5_mean.csv
```

The parent-level truth is:

```python
parent_true[label] = max(segment_true[label])
```

The parent-level probability is generated by a label-specific aggregation rule.

---

## 2. Labels

labels = [

---

## 3. Aggregation functions

| Aggregation | Meaning | Typical behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| `mean` | Average segment probabilities | Conservative; best for stable labels |
| `max` | Maximum segment probability | Sensitive to short evidence but over-predicts |
| `top2mean` | Average of top two segment probabilities | Balanced compromise for sparse evidence |

---

## 5. Why `max` is not used in the final map

Global `max` improved rare transient labels in early diagnostics but damaged the overall prediction set by over-predicting labels. In the mapping bank, the best map used no `max` labels:

```text
mean_count     = 7
max_count      = 0
top2mean_count = 3
```

## 6. Repeated split calibration

Repeated split calibration was useful to discover stable aggregation behaviour:

| Method                                       |   Macro-F1 mean |   Macro-F1 std |   Micro-F1 mean |   Samples-F1 mean |   Exact mean |   Hamming mean ↓ |   Avg pred labels mean |
|:---------------------------------------------|----------------:|---------------:|----------------:|------------------:|-------------:|-----------------:|-----------------------:|
| max fixed                                    |          0.7187 |         0.0171 |          0.82   |            0.8419 |       0.5098 |           0.0632 |                 2.0371 |
| mean fixed                                   |          0.7802 |         0.0071 |          0.9315 |            0.9392 |       0.8371 |           0.0199 |                 1.4274 |
| top2mean fixed                               |          0.8023 |         0.017  |          0.8884 |            0.906  |       0.6927 |           0.0358 |                 1.7377 |
| selected aggregation + fixed threshold       |          0.831  |         0.0135 |          0.9345 |            0.9449 |       0.8368 |           0.0193 |                 1.4674 |
| selected aggregation + calibrated thresholds |          0.8319 |         0.0158 |          0.9288 |            0.9363 |       0.8185 |           0.0208 |                 1.4486 |

But calibrated thresholds were not selected because they did not clearly improve the full corrected-holdout result.

---

## 7. Old TATA-LAWYER maps

Old TATA-LAWYER mappings were relevant because they were trained/evaluated on a related setup. However, when tested on the current v0.9 model probabilities, they did not beat the v0.9 frequency-derived map.

The old per-label thresholds were rejected:

```text
tata_lawyer_optimal_map_fixed_thresholds
Macro-F1   = 0.7284
Micro-F1   = 0.8610
Exact      = 0.6540
Hamming    = 0.0436
```

Final decision:

```text
Use v09_frozen_frequency_plus_gary_mean with fixed threshold 0.5.
```

---

## 8. Recommended reporting text

```text
We apply labelwise parent aggregation to the segment probabilities produced by the v0.8-HCB model. 
Instead of using mean aggregation for all labels, v0.9 assigns each label a frozen aggregation rule selected from mean, max, and top2mean. 
The final map uses mean for seven stable labels and top2mean for three labels, with a fixed threshold of 0.5. 
This improves corrected-holdout Macro-F1 from 0.7801 to 0.8518 while also improving Micro-F1, Exact Match, and Hamming Loss.
```
